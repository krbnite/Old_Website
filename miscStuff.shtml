At one point I thought I'd dive deep into the realm of pure mathematics. While doing my master's in physics, I tried to fit in as many math courses as possible: real/complex analysis, differntiable manifolds, topology, abstract algebra. Mathematics can be addicting, especially when you get to this profound, abstract stuff at the advanced undergraduate / graduate level: one cannot help but feel that there are many parts of mathematics just waiting for applied scientists, engineers, and data scientists to discover and plunder! At that time I began doing (read: trying to do) fractional calculus research with Denis Blackmore. Somehow and some way that got me into operator algebras, quantum groups, and ultimately noncommutative geometry. The basic premise of noncommutative geometry has to do with .... What a cool thought. Something to meditate on.

While getting into all these different areas of mathematics, I tried to attend various seminars and conferences on the subjects. I wanted to immerse myself in it so maybe I'd learn it quicker. Not sure if that happened or not given the heiroglyphic look of the powerpoint presentations barely got better over time, but I had fun doing it.

I attended a week-long workshop on operator algebras and conformal field theory hosted at the University of Oregon this past summer; it was comprised of lectures and many informal discussions in between.

Later on I attended the East Coast Operator Algebra Symposium (ECOAS) at Dartmouth College. You can get an idea of the fun from this synopsis.

Really, I was just into going to mathematics seminars in general and trying to follow along. Just before I left for New Hampshire for ECOAS, I managed to sqeeze in a conference at CUNY Graduate Center on hyperbolic geometry and conformal dynamics to get a taste for other things going on in the world of math.

This was all happening during a period of bibliomania --- i.e., I was buying way too many books.
